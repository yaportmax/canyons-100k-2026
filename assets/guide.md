## Course Sections — Detailed Breakdown

### Section 1: China Wall to Deadwood 1 (Miles 0–10)
- **Terrain:** Mitchell Mine Road (rutted fire road) → singletrack
- **Character:** Rolling; early congestion with two-way traffic
- **Hazards:** Ankle-rolling ruts; mud in wet years
- **Strategy:** GO CONSERVATIVE. Save energy for what's coming.

### Section 2: Deadwood → Devil's Thumb → Swinging Bridge (Miles 10–15)
- **Terrain:** Narrow singletrack; 36 switchbacks on Devil's Thumb descent
- **Character:** Most iconic/dramatic section. Steep descent to river, then steep climb back
- **Hazards:** Bidirectional traffic creates bottlenecks
- **Notable:** Swinging Bridge is a major landmark; bib check by volunteers

### Section 3: Deadwood 2 → Michigan Bluff (Miles 15–24)
- **Terrain:** Mixed singletrack; descent to Eldorado Bridge, then 1,800 ft climb
- **Character:** Hot and exposed sections; five creek crossings
- **Strategy:** Many runners first experience cramping here. Use creek crossings to cool down.

### Section 4: Michigan Bluff → Foresthill (Miles 24–30)
- **Terrain:** Bath Road and Volcano Canyon; creek crossing bottleneck
- **Character:** "Brutal" per multiple reports
- **Notable:** Foresthill = major crew/drop bag point, most festive aid station

### Section 5: Foresthill → Cal 2 / Elevator Shaft (Miles 30–38)
- **Terrain:** Gorgeous forest singletrack → Cal Street
- **Character:** Senseman Hill (mile 34) is extremely steep. Elevator Shaft descent before Cal 2
- **Hazards:** Poison oak prevalent; mosquitoes at Cal 2

### Section 6: Cal 2 → Rucky Chucky / Driver's Flat (Miles 38–48)
- **Terrain:** Gloriously runnable singletrack trending downhill to American River
- **Character:** "Fastest 16-mile section of WS Trail" — sub-7-min miles possible
- **THIS IS THE TRAP:** The runnable terrain tempts you to bank time, but this DESTROYS legs for the return
- **Exposure:** Often the hottest part of the day; very sun-exposed
- **Critical:** The climb from Rucky Chucky to Driver's Flat is where the race truly begins

### Section 7: Driver's Flat → Mammoth Bar (Miles 48–55)
- **Terrain:** Rolling trail along American River
- **Character:** Mentally challenging rollers late in race; exposed to heat

### Section 8: Mammoth Bar → Confluence → No Hands Bridge (Miles 55–60)
- **Terrain:** Rocky; careful footing needed
- **Character:** Likely in darkness for mid-to-back-of-pack
- **Notable:** No Hands Bridge = iconic historic railroad bridge

### Section 9: No Hands Bridge → Auburn Finish (Miles 60–64)
- **Terrain:** Final climb to Robie Point; trail + pavement
- **Character:** Last 3.7 miles significantly uphill. Cross Ann Trason's White Bridge.
- **Strategy:** Crowd energy in Auburn provides a final boost

## Race Strategy

### Pacing
- **#1 mistake: Going out too fast on runnable terrain** (especially miles 30–48)
- Plan RPE targets for each section before the race
- Conservative early pacing is critical for mid-pack runners
- The first 50K is hillier; the second 50K is more runnable but you'll be fatigued
- Front-of-pack positioning matters to avoid bottlenecks at narrow canyon sections

### Nutrition
- **Target 60–100g carbs/hour** (experiment in training)
- Do NOT rely on aid stations — bring your own primary fuel
- Getting behind on calories in the first half is nearly impossible to recover from
- Popular choices: Huma Gels, Tailwind, Spring Energy, Hammer Endurolytes
- Practice eating solid food: rice cakes, bread, boiled potatoes
- Conduct a sweat rate test before race day for personalized electrolyte needs
- Drink aggressively between aid stations (gaps up to 7–9 miles)

### Gear Tips
- **Don't overdress at the start** — arm sleeves > long-sleeve shirt
- Consider 1.5–2L water capacity for the hot Cal 2 → Rucky Chucky section
- Trekking poles: mixed reviews. Helpful on steep loose uphills, dead weight on flats. If you bring them, have a stow plan
- Toe socks to prevent blisters
- Anti-chafe products (Body Glide, Squirrel's Nut Butter)
- Extra hat for dipping in streams

### Creek Crossings
Use them! Soak buffs, hats, and arm sleeves at every crossing for temperature regulation.

### Poison Oak
- Prevalent on trails in April
- Stay on-trail, especially during bathroom breaks
- Carry CoreTex IvyX or dish soap for immediate treatment

## Common Mistakes (from Race Reports)

1. **Under-fueling and under-hydrating** in the first half
2. **Going too fast on runnable descents** (miles 30–48), destroying legs for the return
3. **Insufficient hill training** — relentless climbs and descents punish underprepared quads
4. **Overdressing at the start** and overheating
5. **Relying on aid station food** instead of carrying personal nutrition
6. **Not practicing race-day nutrition** in training
7. **Ignoring creek crossings** as cooling opportunities
8. **Poor positioning at the start** leading to bottlenecks at canyon narrows

## Training Recommendations

### Weekly Structure
- 5 core workouts: speed, strength, tempo/LT, endurance, recovery
- At least 1 rest day per week
- Never consecutive hard sessions
- ~10% weekly volume progression
- Recovery week every 4 weeks

### Key Workouts
- **Long runs:** Build to 5–6 hours; alternate uphill-focused and downhill-focused weekends
- **Back-to-back long runs:** 3-hour Sat + 3-hour Sun
- **Hill work:** Treadmill at 15% grade; continuous downhill training is critical and often neglected
- **LT work:** 5–10 min repeats on 8–12% inclines
- **Milestone runs:** 50K race + 40-mile training run, 4–6 weeks before race

### Taper (Final 2 Weeks)
- Week 1: 70–75% volume
- Week 2 (race week): 20–40% volume
  - Mon: Rest
  - Tue: 30–45 min easy + 4–6 strides
  - Wed: Rest
  - Thu: 30–45 min easy
  - Fri: 20–40 min easy or rest
  - Sat: RACE DAY
